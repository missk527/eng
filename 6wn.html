Topic: The effects of global warming

Notes

  1. Rising global temperatures – Average surface temperatures are increasing, leading to more frequent and intense heatwaves.
  2. Melting ice caps and glaciers – Polar regions and mountainous glaciers are shrinking, reducing freshwater reserves and altering ecosystems.
  3. Sea level rise – Thermal expansion of oceans and melting ice contribute to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities.
  4. More extreme weather – Hurricanes, storms, droughts, and heavy rainfall events are becoming more frequent and severe.
  5. Ocean warming and acidification – Warmer oceans and increased CO₂ absorption damage marine life, including coral reefs and fisheries.
  6. Loss of biodiversity – Species face habitat loss, migration challenges, and extinction as ecosystems rapidly change.
  7. Disruption of agriculture – Shifts in temperature and rainfall patterns affect crop yields, food security, and farming livelihoods.
  8. Increased wildfires – Hotter, drier conditions create more frequent and destructive wildfire seasons.
  9. Public health impacts – Heat-related illnesses, vector-borne diseases, and respiratory problems increase as conditions change.
  10. Economic and social displacement – Damage to infrastructure, loss of livelihoods, and climate migration put pressure on economies and communities.
