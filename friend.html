First-Time Friend Interview Guide

Use this quick guide to meet 3 new classmates, ask friendly questions, and make a positive first impression. Keep it light, listen well, and follow up!

Quick Steps

  1. Warm hello. Smile, say your name, and ask theirs. “Hey, I’m __. What’s your name?”
  2. Easy opener. Use a simple shared-context question. “How are you finding this class so far?”
  3. Common ground. Ask about studies, interests, or hometown.
  4. Follow-up. Reflect what they say and invite more. “You mentioned basketball—do you play or watch?”
  5. Share a bit. Offer a short detail about yourself to balance the chat.
  6. Wrap kindly. Thank them and suggest connecting again.

Conversation Flow (Sample)

You: “Hey, I’m Alex. What’s your name?”

Them: “I’m Sam.”

You: “Nice to meet you, Sam! How are you finding this class so far?”

Them: “… (mentions they like the projects)”

You: “Same here—the projects are fun. Which subject are you most into this term?”

Them: “… (mentions design)”

You: “Oh cool, I’m into that too. Do you follow any designers or creators?”

Wrap-up line: “Thanks for chatting—want to swap contacts or meet up after class sometime?”

Homework: Interview Report (Article Format)

Write a short article about your experience interviewing three classmates. Use the prompts below to structure your report. Suggested length: 450-600 words.

1) Title & Introduction (60–100 words)

  • Give your article a clear title (e.g., “First Conversations: Meeting Three New Classmates”).
  • Briefly explain the task: who you interviewed, where/when it happened, and your goal.

2) Method: How You Interviewed (70–120 words)

  • How did you approach each person? (opening line, setting)
  • What questions did you use first, and why?
  • What listening techniques did you try? (follow-ups, paraphrasing, using their name)

3) Findings: What You Learned (250–350 words)

  • For each classmate, summarize 2–3 key points (studies, interests, goals).
  • Include at least one short quote from each person (use quotation marks).
  • Identify themes across the three interviews (e.g., common hobbies or study tips).

4) Reflection: What Worked & What You’d Improve (120–180 words)

  • Which questions started the best conversations? Why?
  • Where did the conversation stall? How would you fix that next time?
  • What did you learn about yourself as a listener?

5) Conclusion & Next Steps (40–80 words)

  • How will you follow up with these classmates?
  • One action you’ll take to build these new connections.

Submission Checklist

  • Clear title and headings.
  • Word count within 600–900 words.
  • At least 3 short quotes (one per classmate).
  • Proofread for clarity and tone (friendly, respectful).

Optional: Add a brief appendix with your question list.

Grading Rubric (Total: 50 points)

  • Structure & Organization – 10 points
    Clear title, headings, and logical flow.
  • Method Description – 10 points
    Explanation of approach, question choice, and listening strategies.
  • Findings – 15 points
    Depth and clarity in reporting classmates’ answers; includes required quotes.
  • Reflection – 10 points
    Insightful analysis of what worked, what to improve, and self-awareness.
  • Language & Presentation – 5 points
    Grammar, tone (friendly and respectful), and word count accuracy.
