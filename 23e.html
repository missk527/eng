Practice: Descriptive Sentences (Rainstorm)
  1. Dark storm clouds loom heavily over the valley, swallowing the fading daylight.
  2. Forked lightning slices through the sky, briefly exposing the trembling forest below.
  3. Torrential rain hammers the ground relentlessly, turning narrow streams into rushing rivers.
  4. Fierce winds tear through the trees, forcing their branches to bow in surrender.
  5. Thunder explodes across the hills like the roar of an angry giant.
  6. Raindrops fall like icy needles, piercing the soaked grass and muddy soil.
  7. The swollen river surges forward, dragging leaves and broken branches in its powerful grip.
  8. A cold mist rises from the earth, wrapping the valley in a cloak of mystery.
  9. Shadows leap wildly across the landscape as lightning shatters the darkness again and again.
  10. The flooded ground glistens beneath the stormy sky like shattered glass.
  11. The forest seems to hold its breath while the storm claims control of the land.
  12. Water races through the field, transforming peaceful nature into a restless battlefield.
  13. The rainstorm stands like a ruthless ruler, commanding every corner of the valley.
  14. Standing beside the raging river, I feel both terrified and awed by nature’s raw power.
  15. This violent storm serves as a powerful reminder of how fragile humans are before nature.
