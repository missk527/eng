Should homework be banned?

Video link: https://youtu.be/2PuuUF9MLV0

Notes
  1. Reduces student stress – Excessive homework increases pressure, anxiety, and burnout, especially for younger students.
  2. Limits free time – Homework takes away time for rest, hobbies, family interaction, and personal development.
  3. Causes inequality – Not all students have equal access to a quiet study space, parental help, or resources at home.
  4. Questionable academic benefits – Research shows little to no academic improvement from homework at the primary level.
  5. Encourages rote learning – Homework often focuses on repetition rather than creativity, critical thinking, or real understanding.
  6. Affects mental health – Long hours of homework can lead to sleep deprivation and negatively impact emotional well-being.
  7. Shifts responsibility to parents – Homework often forces parents to act as teachers, which can create family conflict.
  8. Reduces interest in learning – Too much homework can make learning feel like a burden rather than an enjoyable process.
  9. School time should be sufficient – Effective teaching during school hours should eliminate the need for extra work at home.
  10. Alternative learning methods exist – Projects, class discussions, and hands-on activities can replace homework more effectively.
